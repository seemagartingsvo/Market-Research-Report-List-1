<p><p>The medical device market features two primary types of water chillers: air-cooled and water-cooled water chillers. Air-cooled water chillers utilize ambient air for cooling, making them easier to install and maintain, ideal for facilities with space constraints. On the other hand, water-cooled chillers use water from a cooling tower or a similar source, offering higher efficiency and performance over larger systems. Both types play a critical role in maintaining optimal temperatures for sensitive medical devices and laboratory equipment.</p></p>
